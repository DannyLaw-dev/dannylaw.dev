Privacy Policy

How the websites at dannylaw.dev and its subdomains handle your data. These are personal, hobby projects built and run by one person — this policy is written plainly to match.

Last updated: 1 June 2026 Controller: Danny Lawrence Jurisdiction: United Kingdom

The short version

No analytics, no tracking, no advertising, and no first-party cookies run on any of these sites. Most of them collect nothing about you at all. The one that stores real data is the inspection app, which is a personal work tool you have to sign into. Where a site does send data anywhere, it's spelled out in the table below.

Who runs these sites

I'm Danny Lawrence, an individual in the United Kingdom. I build and run these sites in my spare time. For anything in this policy — including any data request — contact me at dannylaw.dev@gmail.com. I am the data controller for the personal data described here.

Cookies and local storage

None of these sites set first-party cookies, and none run tracking or analytics cookies. So there's no cookie-consent banner — there's nothing non-essential to consent to.

Some sites use your browser's local storage to function — for example, the inspection app keeps your working data on your device, and the spares tool remembers your label inputs. This is strictly necessary for those tools to work, and the data stays in your browser unless described otherwise above.

Third parties may set their own cookies when you use a feature that involves them — Google when you sign in or connect Google Sheets, and Cloudflare when it gates access to the private tools. Those cookies are governed by Google's and Cloudflare's own privacy policies.

The services that process data for me

I don't have my own servers for most of this — these tools rely on a few third-party providers acting as processors:

  • Google (Firebase / Firestore, Hosting, Sign-in, Sheets, Fonts) — hosts the sites, runs sign-in for the inspection app, and stores the app's data. The app's database is configured to store data in Europe. Google is a global company and may process data outside the UK under its own legal safeguards.
  • Cloudflare — handles DNS for the domain and the access gate on the private tools.
  • jsDelivr and cdnjs — content-delivery networks that serve some code libraries; they see your IP address when a page loads those files.
  • n8n and an AI model provider — only for the wexley chat toy, which sends your messages to them to generate replies.

Why I'm allowed to process this data (legal basis)

  • The inspection app: it's my own tool and I'm its main user — processing is on the basis of legitimate interests in running it. Where it stores another person's email (a manager/viewer or preparer you invite), that rests on legitimate interests and on their choosing to use it.
  • Connecting Google Sheets: your explicit consent, given through Google's permission screen. You can disconnect at any time.
  • The wexley chat: your consent, given by choosing to send a message.

How long data is kept

Inspection-app data is kept until you delete it; archived days are retained as history until removed. Anything in your browser's local storage stays until you clear it. Wexley chat messages aren't stored by the site, though the third-party services it calls may keep their own logs under their policies.

Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to access the personal data held about you, to correct it, to have it deleted, to restrict or object to its processing, and to data portability. To exercise any of these, email dannylaw.dev@gmail.com.

For the inspection app specifically: you can export your data from within the app, and I can delete your account and its stored data on request. You can also clear the on-device data yourself by clearing your browser storage for the site.

If you think your data has been mishandled, you can complain to the UK's Information Commissioner's Office (ico.org.uk).

Children

These sites aren't directed at children and aren't intended for use by anyone under 13.
